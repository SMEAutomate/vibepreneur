# Week 3: The distribution problem nobody warns you about

**Subject:** You built it. Nobody came. Now what?

---

There's a specific moment that kills most new products. It's not a bad idea. It's not poor execution. It's the Tuesday after launch when you check your analytics and see four visitors, three of which were you on different devices.

**Building is the easy part now.** The hard part is reaching buyers before they've already decided to buy something else.

Most first-time builders make the same mistake: they finish the product, then start thinking about distribution. By then, they've already burned their energy, their timeline, and their motivation. Distribution isn't a phase that comes after building. It runs in parallel from day one.

**What effective distribution actually looks like for expertise-based solutions:**

It's not viral loops, growth hacking, or spending money on ads you can't afford. It's structured outreach to the people who already have the problem you solve, through channels they already trust.

Three distribution channels consistently work for solutions built from professional expertise:

1. **Targeted content in niche communities.** Not "content marketing" but specific, problem-aware content placed where your buyers already gather. One post in the right Slack group outperforms 50 blog posts nobody reads.

2. **Cold outreach with a free diagnostic.** Instead of selling, offer to assess the problem for free. A "reporting efficiency audit" or "onboarding cost assessment" opens doors that a sales pitch never will.

3. **Partnership with adjacent service providers.** Consultants, coaches, and agencies who serve your buyers but don't offer your specific solution. They become referral channels with zero customer acquisition cost.

### 3 moves you can make this week:

1. **Pick one niche community from your list last week.** Write a 200-word post about the problem you solve. Not a pitch. An insight. Something that makes people think "this person actually understands my problem."

2. **Draft a one-page "diagnostic" version of your solution.** A simple assessment or audit that someone could complete in 10 minutes and get immediate value from. This becomes your lead magnet and your foot in the door.

3. **Identify 5 service providers who serve your target buyers.** Consultants, agencies, coaches. Reach out to one with a simple message: "I'm building [solution] for [buyer]. Do you see this problem with your clients?"
