# Weekly Email Template — Vibepreneur

## Subject Line Format

[Problem insight] — [unexpected angle or contrarian take]

Example: "The skill that made you successful is now your biggest risk"

## Structure

### Opening (2-3 sentences)

- Start with a sharp observation about the professional world
- Name a specific problem or pattern the reader recognises
- No "Hi [Name]" — start with substance

### Problem Insight (3-5 sentences)

- Dig into WHY this problem exists
- Use specific numbers, timeframes, or scenarios
- Connect it to the reader's experience without being generic

### Solution Angle (3-4 sentences)

- Introduce a different way to think about the problem
- Frame it as turning expertise into ownership
- Keep it practical — what could someone do about this?

### Distribution Wedge (2-3 sentences)

- Show how the solution reaches buyers
- Name specific channels, communities, or strategies
- Make it feel executable, not theoretical

### 3 Moves (numbered list)

- Three specific actions the reader could take this week
- Each should be concrete and measurable
- Progress from easy to meaningful

### Product Update (2 sentences, max)

- Brief mention of what we shipped or what's coming
- Link to roadmap or feature page
- Keep it subtle — this isn't a sales pitch

### Sign-off

"See you next week."
— The Vibepreneur Team

## Tone Rules

- Authority without arrogance
- Strategic urgency without hype
- Specific over generic
- Never start with "In today's fast-paced world..."
- Avoid: "AI", "disrupt", "game-changer", "unlock"
- Use: "modern tools", "systems", "structured approach", "leverage"
