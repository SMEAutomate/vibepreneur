# Week 1: The skill that made you successful is now your biggest risk

**Subject:** The skill that made you successful is now your biggest risk

---

The most valuable people in most companies are the ones who fix problems nobody else notices. They optimise workflows. They bridge gaps between teams. They know exactly where things break.

And they get rewarded for it with more responsibilities, not more ownership.

**Here's the pattern nobody talks about.** The deeper your expertise becomes, the more dependent you are on someone else's platform to use it. Your knowledge appreciates in value while your leverage stays flat. You become more skilled and more replaceable at the same time.

This isn't a motivation problem. It's a structural one.

The professionals who break out of this pattern do something specific: they package what they already know into something the market can buy, without them being present to deliver it every time.

**The distribution angle most people miss:** You don't need an audience to start. You need a buyer profile and three channels where those buyers already look for solutions. LinkedIn thought leadership, niche community partnerships, and targeted outreach cover 80% of early traction for expertise-based solutions.

### 3 moves you can make this week:

1. **Write down the 3 problems you solve most often at work.** Not your job title. The actual problems. "New hires take 3 months to get productive" is better than "I do onboarding."

2. **For each problem, name who outside your company would pay to solve it.** Job title, company size, industry. If you can't name the buyer, the problem isn't specific enough.

3. **Search LinkedIn for 10 people with that buyer profile.** Look at what they post about, what they complain about, what questions they ask. This is market research that takes 15 minutes.
