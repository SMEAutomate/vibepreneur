# Vibepreneur — Editorial Voice & Copy Guidelines

## Voice

**Balanced but sharp.** We speak with calm authority and strategic urgency. We don't hype. We don't hedge. We state what's true and what to do about it.

**Think:** A senior advisor who's seen it all and gives direct, useful guidance without posturing.

## Tone Spectrum

| Situation     | Tone                                 |
| ------------- | ------------------------------------ |
| Homepage hero | Confident, aspirational but grounded |
| How It Works  | Clear, methodical, educational       |
| Pricing       | Straightforward, value-focused       |
| Roadmap       | Transparent, honest, forward-looking |
| Waitlist      | Warm but professional                |
| Weekly emails | Sharp, insightful, strategic         |

## Banned Phrases

Never use these in any Vibepreneur copy:

| Banned                      | Why                                      | Use Instead                                 |
| --------------------------- | ---------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------- |
| AI-powered                  | Overused, triggers skepticism            | "modern tools", "structured systems"        |
| Micro-SaaS                  | Jargon, alienates non-technical audience | "scalable solution"                         |
| Agents/Orchestration        | Technical jargon                         | (don't reference)                           |
| LLM / Large Language Model  | Technical jargon                         | (don't reference)                           |
| Disrupt / Disruption        | Cliché                                   | "shift", "change"                           |
| Game-changer                | Empty hype                               | (be specific about what changes)            |
| Unlock your potential       | Generic motivation                       | (be specific about what they'll do)         |
| In today's fast-paced world | Every bad blog post                      | (just state the problem)                    |
| Leverage AI                 | Buzzword combo                           | "use modern tools"                          |
| 10x / 100x                  | Hype language                            | Use real numbers                            |
| Passive income              | MLM connotation                          | "scalable revenue"                          |
| Side hustle                 | Trivialising                             | "scalable solution", "professional project" |
| Solopreneur                 | Too niche/trendy                         | "professional", "builder"                   |
| No-code                     | Implies limitation                       | (don't mention code at all)                 |

## Writing Rules

1. **Lead with the problem, not the feature.** Always start from what the reader experiences, not what the product does.

2. **Be specific over generic.** "Reduces onboarding time from 90 days to 30 days" beats "improves onboarding efficiency."

3. **Use active voice.** "You build the solution" not "The solution is built by you."

4. **Short sentences for impact.** Long sentences for explanation. Vary rhythm.

5. **No exclamation marks.** Ever. They undermine authority.

6. **Numbers are specific.** Don't say "hundreds of" when you can say "200+". Don't say "significant" when you can say "40%".

7. **CTAs are direct.** "Join the Waitlist" not "Click Here to Learn More About Joining."

8. **The reader is smart.** Don't over-explain. Don't patronise. Assume they've read a business book or two.

## Copy Structure Patterns

### Section Headlines

- Statement of fact: "Building is step one. Distribution is the real skill."
- Question the reader relates to: "What would you build from 10 years of expertise?"
- Imperative + benefit: "Adapt early. Win long-term."

### Body Copy

- Open with observation or problem
- Bridge to insight (the "why nobody does this" part)
- Close with what the reader can do about it

### Feature Descriptions

- Name (short, noun-based)
- One-sentence benefit statement
- 2-3 sentence explanation of what it does and why it matters

### CTA Blocks

- Headline (aspirational but grounded)
- One sentence of context
- Single primary button
- Optional secondary action

## Email Voice

Emails are sharper than web copy. They read like strategic memos from someone who's been in the trenches:

- Open with a bold statement or observation
- Get to the point in the first paragraph
- Include one actionable insight the reader can use regardless of whether they buy
- Close with subtle product mention (never more than 2 sentences)
- Sign off consistently: "See you next week. — The Vibepreneur Team"
