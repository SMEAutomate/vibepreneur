# Vibepreneur. Strategic Dashboard Mock Specification

## Overview

The dashboard mock is a visual component shown in the homepage hero section. It represents the Vibepreneur platform's strategic output. not a technical admin panel but a decision-making interface for professionals building solutions from their expertise.

## Design Philosophy

- **Strategic, not technical.** No code, no terminal aesthetics, no API responses.
- **Outcome-focused.** Every panel answers "what should I do next?" not "what happened."
- **Calm confidence.** Clean, white background, muted colours, clear hierarchy.

## Panels

### 1. Opportunity Score (87/100)

**What it represents:** A composite score indicating how strong the market opportunity is for a given solution concept. Factors in demand signals, competition density, buyer accessibility, and the builder's expertise fit.

**Why it matters:** Prevents professionals from pursuing weak opportunities. A score above 70 suggests the opportunity is worth full development.

**Visual:** Large number with /100 suffix. Emerald green when high.

### 2. Buyer Clarity (High)

**What it represents:** How well-defined the target buyer is. Considers specificity of job title, company size, industry, and whether the buyer has purchasing authority.

**Why it matters:** Vague buyer definitions are the #1 reason solutions fail to gain traction. This metric forces specificity.

**Visual:** Text label (Low/Medium/High). Brand blue when High.

### 3. Channel Fit (3 strong)

**What it represents:** How many distribution channels have strong alignment with the target buyer. Evaluates community presence, content opportunity, outreach feasibility, and partnership potential.

**Why it matters:** A solution with no clear channels to reach buyers is dead on arrival. This metric validates distribution before building.

**Visual:** Count with "strong" suffix. Brand blue.

### 4. Launch Readiness (72%)

**What it represents:** Percentage completion of the go-to-market checklist. Covers positioning, messaging, channel setup, outreach templates, and content assets.

**Why it matters:** Shows exactly how much work remains before launch. Prevents premature launches and endless building.

**Visual:** Percentage. Amber when below 80%, emerald when above.

### 5. Next 3 GTM Moves

**What it represents:** The three highest-priority actions the professional should take next to move their solution toward market. Prioritised by impact and effort.

**Why it matters:** Eliminates decision paralysis. Professionals always know exactly what to do next.

**Seed content:**

1. Publish positioning article on LinkedIn
2. Launch free assessment tool for lead capture
3. Run cold outreach to 50 target buyers

## Future Dashboard Panels (Post-MVP)

These are planned for the full platform but not shown in the mock:

- **Traction Velocity**. Week-over-week growth in early signals (signups, replies, conversations)
- **Competitive Position**. Where the solution sits relative to alternatives
- **Revenue Readiness**. How close the solution is to monetisation
- **Growth Loop Health**. Whether sustainable growth mechanics are in place
